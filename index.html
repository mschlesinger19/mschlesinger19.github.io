My Timeline

  • August 2011 - May 2015

    University of Miami

    I graduated from the University of Miami with a 3.66 GPA and a Bachelor's of Science double majoring in Computer Engineering and Computer Science. While I was not attending class I was the Treasurer of my fraternity, Pi Kappa Phi; I was the re-founding Vice President of the Honor Society for Computer Disciplines, Upsilon Pi Epsilon; and was the Corresponding Secretary of IEEE's Eta Kappa Nu, the Electrical and Computer Engineering Honor Society.

  • August 2011 - May 2015

    Facility Supervisor - Patti and Allan Herbert Wellness Center

    Starting a a lifeguard on the Friday morning opening shift, I quickly rose up the ranks to become a Facility Supervisor. I supervised up to 25 employees and maintained the safety of both the patrons and the employees of the facility.

  • September 2012 - May 2015

    Special Events Coordinator and Tour Guide

    University of Miami Admissions

    Besides giving tours to prospective students and their families, I also organized the two biggest admissions events of the year, Winter and Spring Open Houses. Speaking at these events and on tours every week made me realize that my weakness at the time, public speaking, would soon turn into one of my strengths.

  • Summers 2013 and 2014

    Controls Engineer Intern

    Siemens Industry, Inc.

    As a part of my internship at Siemens, I collaborated with sales industry and project managers to design the most efficient HVAC systems for high-rises, hospitals, stadiums, and public facilities using AutoCAD, SAP software, and other tools. I quickly experienced the process from the initial sale of a system to the finished product walk-through.

  • August 2015 - May 2017

    Software Engineer

    Lockheed Martin

    Between 3 projects at 2 different sites, I have seen all different aspects of the development life cycle. I have created 2 separate regression testing suites using Selenium and Robot Framework to detect any code breakage overnight for a product being released and verifying requirements for an IRAD development. Also was involved in developing a new user interface for a government asset scheduling system with back-end ties to other teams' software and a communications system displaying the statuses of hardware through SNMP. Planned community outreach events as a member of the Diversity and Inclusion Council.

    Technologies used: Java, AngularJS, Git, Mercurial, Atlassian, AGILE, Slenium, Robot Framework, Python

  • May 2017 - Present

    Embedded Software Engineer

    Northrop Grumman

    Sat at customer site and participated in an AGILE environment developing a user interface for a military programmable radio.

I created an Android application for realtors to assist them in recording estimates for rental and flip properties as they do their "initial walk-throughs". With a local database, users can easily save and edit previous entries if they have any changes. Best Windows Phone Application - UHack Microsoft's Grand Prize

A few classmates and myself created a Window's phone application that won Microsoft's Grand Prize at the University of Miami's 24-hour Hackathon. This application is like Untapped (which was not popular at the time) where you can search a beer database (API found online) to view the beer's description, ABV, location, average user rating, and even save your own rating and notes about the beer for your own reference. We attempted to implement label recognition as a search cirteria but it was not 100% successful due to the time.

  • Location: Coral Gables, FL - University of Miami UHack Competition
  • Date: Spring 2014
